HIAA0063 Midterm Exam Review

Definitions

The Logic of Objects

Maria Martinez. Image sequence of Maria's process. Photography. 1930s. Mexican.

  • Cooke coined the term, the idea to look deeply at the object itself, and not what we believe it to be. Process by which to understand an object.
  • To understand an object on its own terms, begin with materials and techniques of fabrication and decoration, rather than project an ahistorical interpretation based on contemporary taste of prejudice.
  • The focus on paying attention to what one sees and analyzes visually, rather than judging on meaning and culmination.
  • Emphasis on materiality, realization, and then interchange.

Still Life

Clara Peeters. Still Life with Cheese, Almonds, and Pretzels. Oil on canvas. 1615. Flemish.

  • Barkan describes still life as "objects that don't move of speak, a visual entity made from a group of objects, nothing altering design, assemblage".
  • "A deliberate arraignment."
  • A representation of food (statistically, the most) or objects, but often containing a stronger symbolism: mimesis and often painted with verisimilitude depicting food exactly how it is.
  • Accessible paintings because they are often of familiar objects.
  • Took different forms:
    • Xenia (Greek attempt at photo realism), hospitality.
    • Stilleven, Dutch dramaticized displays of patriotism.
    • Bodegon, Spanish monastic works. High contrast, anorexic.

Cacao and Chocolatero

Unknown. Chocolate Jar (Chocolatero).Tin-glazed earthenware, iron. ca 1700. Mexican Puebla.

  • Cacao:
    • Mesoamerican word. Powerful good heavily traded and visualized in Maya culture. Contains amphetamines and other stimulants.
    • Developed originally by the Olmecs. Needs a humid and warm environment.
    • Anthropomorphized as deities, notions of rebirth and cycles. Used as currency.
    • Many vessels showed stories, inscriptions, gods, and the use of Cacao in culture, as a drink or other forms.
  • Chocolatero:
    • Chocolate jars. Sometimes with iron lids that locked, given cacao was valuable.
    • Puebla style of earthenware mixed porcelain-like elements: blue and white colors, but with Mexican imagery, like Quetzal birds.
    • Used for the storage and containing of chocolate and chocolate beans.

Architectural Plans

Charbagh

  • Literally translates to "four garden". Divided by two intersecting channels of water to create quadrants of cultivation.
  • Fountain in the middle. Lined by walls, arcades, and usually a pavilion across the lines on the sides.
  • Small lots of plants arranged geometrically, big focus on mosaic, tile. Mix of trees and plants.
  • Serence oaises in the middle of barren regions, to be walked through peacefully or be a scenic place.

Wabi Tea

  • Rustic and simple places, full of ritual and cultural heritage. Founded on the Wabi styles by Rikyu.
  • Measured by number of mats in the house, usually relatively small. Had to crawl to enter under small door. Different entrances for guests and host. Choreographed ceremony from start to end.
  • Mizuya was water preparation room.
  • Tokonoma was raised alcove with hanging scroll and flower arrangement (ancestral area). Central hearth to brew the tea.

Ottoman Coffeehouse

  • New social houses, for people to go gather and escape the home. Stimulated by coffee.
  • Courtyard structure, fountain in the middle with raised platform (Divan) around it.
  • Separate sitting areas that overlook the main room for intimate gatherings (the top Divan).
  • Very much a social center. A lot of seating room around, often with an open fireplace (the semi circle).
